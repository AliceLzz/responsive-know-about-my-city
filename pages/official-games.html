    • The Grape Games

    "Festa da Uva" (Grape Festival) is a traditional event that takes place in Caxias do Sul, Brazil. It is a celebration of the region's Italian heritage, culture, and its significant role in grape and wine production. The festival has been held since 1931 and has become one of the most important and popular events in the country. The Festa da Uva occurs every two years, typically in February or March, during the grape harvest season. It lasts for approximately two weeks and attracts visitors from all over Brazil and even from abroad. The festival showcases the local agricultural, industrial, and cultural aspects of Caxias do Sul and the surrounding region.

    During the festival, the city comes alive with colorful parades, exhibitions, concerts, folk dances, and other cultural performances. One of the main highlights of the event is the allegoric parade, where elaborately decorated floats depict different themes related to the grape and wine industry, Italian culture, and the local community. The parades also feature traditional music, dance groups, and participants wearing traditional costumes. Another significant aspect of the Festa da Uva is the exhibition and competition of grapes, wines, and other agricultural products. Local winemakers and grape producers showcase their best products, allowing visitors to taste and appreciate the quality of the regional wines.

    Food plays a central role in the festival as well. Visitors can indulge in a variety of Italian and regional dishes, including pasta, polenta, grilled meats, and delicious desserts. Local wineries offer wine tastings, allowing attendees to experience the flavors of the renowned wines produced in the region. The Festa da Uva is not only a celebration of the grape harvest and wine production but also a way to honor the cultural heritage and contributions of Italian immigrants to the local community. It is a festive and joyous event that brings people together to enjoy the traditions, flavors, and hospitality of Caxias do Sul.
