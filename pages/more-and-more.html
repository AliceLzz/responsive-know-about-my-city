Caxias do Sul has several interesting curiosities that make it a unique and notable city. Here are a few: Italian Influence: Caxias do Sul has a strong Italian influence due to the significant number of Italian immigrants who settled in the region in the late 19th century. This heritage is still visible in the city's architecture, traditions, cuisine, and cultural events. Vineyards and Wine Production: Caxias do Sul is part of the Serra Gaucha wine region, known for its vineyards and wine production. The city is surrounded by beautiful landscapes dotted with vineyards, and wine tasting tours are popular among visitors.

Industry and Entrepreneurship: Caxias do Sul is recognized as one of the main industrial hubs in Brazil, particularly in the metalworking sector. The city is home to numerous manufacturing companies and has a reputation for entrepreneurship and innovation. Snowfall: While not a regular occurrence, Caxias do Sul occasionally experiences snowfall during the winter months (June to August). It is one of the few cities in Brazil where you can witness snow, creating a unique and picturesque atmosphere.

Gastronomic Capital: Caxias do Sul has been recognized as the "Gastronomic Capital of Brazil" due to its vibrant culinary scene. The city offers a wide range of restaurants, cafés, and food establishments that showcase both Italian and regional cuisines. Festivals and Events: Besides the Festa da Uva, Caxias do Sul hosts several other festivals and events throughout the year. These include the Feira do Livro (Book Fair), Festiqueijo (Cheese Festival), and Oktoberfest, which celebrate literature, cheese, and German culture, respectively.

Historic Train Ride: The Maria Fumaça Train is a popular tourist attraction in Caxias do Sul. This steam locomotive offers a scenic ride through the countryside, passing through vineyards and small towns, providing a nostalgic experience for visitors. Château Lacave: Located in Caxias do Sul, Château Lacave is an impressive castle-like structure that houses a winery and a wine cellar. It is a picturesque destination for wine enthusiasts and visitors looking to explore the region's wine culture. These are just a few curiosities that make Caxias do Sul an intriguing city to visit. Its blend of Italian heritage, industrial prowess, and natural beauty offers a unique and diverse experience for both locals and tourists.
